What is your interest in IT?

My personal interest in studying Information Technology stems from a desire to pursue a career in either cyber security or software development. I enjoy the aspects of problem solving that come with coding as well as the natural challenge of constantly updating your system to protect against ever evolving cyber threats and the creativity afforded when designing a new program. I have always had a benign interest in IT to the extent of regularly providing relatives from computer advice and tech support, as well as a base level of knowledge in networking and databases, but my real interest began in my teens when I built my first custom desktop computer. The process of installing new programs and learning the ins and outs of my personal system fascinated me and I have been hooked ever since.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

I chose to study at RMIT as it came highly recommended in the field of Information Technology. Beyond that I am motivated to further build on my knowledge of all things IT. I am particularly interested in learning to write code and to network with figures in the industry to give myself a strong opportunity for employment on completion of my course. I also was attracted to the city campus and the opportunity to study away from home in Melbourne. Moving to the city presented the opportunity to step out of my comfort zone whilst creating more opportunities for networking and future employment prospects than can be found in regional Victoria

What do you expect to learn during your studies?

While studying IT my learning priority is to focus on technical skills such as becoming fluent in different, essential programming languages such as Python, CSS, HTML, as well as developing the skills suitable for a career cyber security or software development. I hope that along with the technical skills and knowledge I will also have ample opportunity for building relationships with people already operating in the industry and even potentially to have the option of an internship further into my course.
